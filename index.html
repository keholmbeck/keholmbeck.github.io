About Me

I like numbers - specifically, identifying patterns in numbers. The problem solving skills one learns in studying mathematics has levels of both rigor and creativity that allows us to approach different types of problems with efficiency.

Having been employed at Tempest Technologies for about 5 years, I have had the opportunity to work on many different types of problems, including optimal control, agent-based modeling, and image processing.

Officially, my job involves mathematical modeling, analysis of those models, and software development. But really, I get to explore patterns and find elegant solutions to problems. Basically, it's all about solving puzzles -- but doing so efficiently and robustly.

I graduated magna cum laude from the applied mathematics program at Loyola Marymount University after transferring from El Camino Community College.

Currently I am in an MS program in applied mathematics at CSULB.
